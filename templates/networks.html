Network explorer

Soviet Journals Reconnected uses bibliographical metadata to analyze periodicals as social communities. Network visualizations show which editors worked together in one or several journals. Networks of co-publication make tangible to what degree the magazines facilitated the separate development of literary communities during the period of late socialism. The network visualizations were created with the software Gephi and are integrated into this Flask website, using Raphael Velt's JavaScript GEXF Viewer.

I. Moscow-Based Editors Between 1958 and 1972

Between 1958 and 1972, around 180 editors worked in the six Moscow-based journals currently covered by the Soviet Journals Reconnected Database. This network visualization shows the color-coded communities of editors that collaborated in one or several editorial offices.

The interactive network visualization allows you to explore the positions of individual editors, some of which managed to bridge several journals and literary communities within the decade. Click here to look at the network.
